Algorithm Visualizer Quick sort

Quick sort is a popular sorting algorithm that follows the divide-and-conquer strategy. It works by selecting a pivot element from the array and partitioning the other elements into two sub-arrays according to whether they are less than or greater than the pivot. The sub-arrays are then recursively sorted. Unlike merge sort, quick sort sorts in place, requiring only a small amount of additional memory. However, its performance depends on the choice of the pivot, and in the worst-case scenario, it can have a time complexity of O(n^2). Nevertheless, on average, quick sort exhibits a time complexity of O(n log n), making it efficient for sorting large datasets.
