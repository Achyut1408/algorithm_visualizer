Algorithm Visualizer Knuth-Morris-Pratt

The Knuth-Morris-Pratt (KMP) algorithm is used for pattern matching in strings. It improves on the brute-force approach by avoiding unnecessary character comparisons. It achieves this by precomputing a prefix function that helps determine the maximum length of the proper suffix of the substring that is also a prefix of the pattern. This information allows the algorithm to shift the pattern more intelligently, avoiding comparisons that were already matched in the previous iterations. This results in a linear time complexity of O(n + m), where n is the length of the text and m is the length of the pattern, making it efficient for large text searches.
